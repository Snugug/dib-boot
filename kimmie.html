About

Kimmie is a visual designer and artist based in New York City, NY.

Her father—a South Vietnamese fighter pilot—emigrated to America, and by stroke of luck ran into her mother in a refugee camp. Sometime in the 90s, he taught Kimmie her ABCs on a machine running DOS. Her mother taught Kimmie to draw mermaids to keep her from rearranging the furniture. She was only trying to optimize the flow of the room befor better TV-viewing!

In 2004, Kimmie took a Journalism class and learned how to thumbnail newspaper layouts. It was a match made in Pagemaker.

Her teacher insisted on the following:

"Design! Don't Decorate!"

This has worked out OK—except Fall 2008, when she made her first site-specific art installation.
